Cardiovascular diseases (CVD) are a group of disorders affecting the heart and blood vessels. These diseases can lead to serious health issues such as heart attacks, strokes, and other circulatory system conditions. Below, we explain more about these conditions:

1. Definition

Cardiovascular diseases are a group of disorders affecting the heart and blood vessels. The most common include coronary artery disease (which can trigger a heart attack), strokes (CVA), heart failure, arrhythmias, among others.

2. Main Causes

The most common causes of cardiovascular diseases include:

High blood pressure (hypertension): Increases the risk of damaging blood vessels and the heart.

High cholesterol: Cholesterol plaques can accumulate in arteries, restricting blood flow.

Smoking: Tobacco use contributes to narrowing blood vessels, increasing the risk of atherosclerosis.

Diabetes: High blood sugar levels can damage blood vessels, increasing cardiovascular risk.

Sedentary lifestyle: Lack of physical activity increases the risk of developing heart diseases.

Unhealthy diet: Diets high in saturated fats, sugars, and salt increase the likelihood of cardiovascular problems.

3. Risk Factors

Several factors can increase the risk of developing cardiovascular disease. Among them are:

Older age: The risk increases as we age.

Family history of heart disease: If a family member has had heart problems, the risk is higher.

Gender: Men have a higher risk, but women can also be affected, especially after menopause.

Chronic stress: Ongoing stress can increase the risk of heart disease.

4. Why is it important to prevent them?

Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death in many countries, but the good news is that they are preventable. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle, detecting health problems early, and receiving appropriate treatment can significantly reduce the risk of developing heart diseases.

In summary: Cardiovascular diseases are serious conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels, but through proper prevention and a healthy life, it is possible to reduce the risk and live a fuller life.
